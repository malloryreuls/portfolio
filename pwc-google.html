PwC / Google for Work Alliance

Reinventing the future of retail
Philosophie 2016
  • Role: Product & UX Design
  • Duration: February 2016 - June 2016
  • Philosophie: Case Study
  • View Demo Video
The Problem

In 2015, PwC and Google formed an alliance to find a way to sell Google Apps for Work licenses to a variety of industries. After limited success with pitch decks, PwC hired Phiosophie to build working prototypes of integrated Google for Work services, with a focus on a specific industry.

The Ask

Find out everything there is to know about Retail. Create a solution leveraging Google Apps for Work to improve it.

The Personas

Enrique, the Store Manager. Enrique has been managing the Santa Monica "Big Box" store for 4+ years. His primary responsiblity is managing inventory, reducing theft, and ensuring his staff has enough information to do their jobs.

Ariella, the Store Associate. Ariella was recently hired at the Santa Monica "Big Box." She is a full-time student at Santa Monica City College and part-time employee. This is just a job for Ariella. When she isn't working or at school, she enjoys taking photos with her iPhone.

Jenny, the Customer. Jenny only goes into stores when she happens to be in the area and needs something specific. She chooses to shop in store versus online when it is a bigger ticket item or clothing. It is very important for her to see what the item looks and feels like. That way she can play with or try on the item, and get an idea of what it might look like in her own home.

The Solution

Store Manager Dashboard: We set out to empower Enrique to be less reactive and more proactive. By giving him detailed insight into the items that are trending in his area and access to ad spend to promote popular items with local Google Ads.

Store Associate App and Watch: In our research, we found that most store associates were detached from their role and didn't know what they needed to do to advance in the company. They also felt very uncomfortable approaching customers in the store and wished there was a better way to know if a customer needed help.

Customer Google Now notifications and in-store chat bot: Most customers conduct online research before going to a store to purchase a big-ticket item. We offer price alert notifications through Google Now, that notify customers when an item they were previously researching online is available in a store near them. Once the cusotmer enters the store, we connect them with an associate who is knowledgebale about the particular item they are interested in through a chat bot.

Key Takeaways

We conducted 25 customer interviews in the first week of the project. Over the course of the next 6 weeks we met with 3-5 users per week. Our original assumptions about the personas evolved on a weekly basis. By the end of the engagement we had a rock-solid demo that we then presented at the Google offices in Mexico City. PwC and Google are currently pitching our demo to retail companies around the world.

Working with a disparate team was quite challenging on this project. To increase the amount of visibility they had on our progress, we made daily videos that were sent off to the Product Owner and management team to help keep them in the loop.
